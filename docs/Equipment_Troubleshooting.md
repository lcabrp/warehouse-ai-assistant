# Equipment Troubleshooting

## Overview
This guide provides level-1 support steps for common warehouse equipment failures, spanning handheld devices to heavy material handling machinery.

## RF Scanner Issues
If an RF scanner fails to read barcodes:
1. Visually inspect the barcode for damage or smudging.
2. Ensure the laser lens is clean.
3. Perform a hard reset by holding the power button for 10 seconds.
If it still will not scan, log a 'Scanner Issue' exception and swap the device at the IT depot.

## Label Printer Problems
If a label printer is skipping labels or printing faintly:
1. Check that the ribbon and label roll are properly aligned and not wrinkled.
2. Clean the printhead using a designated alcohol swab.
3. Recalibrate the printer by holding the 'Feed' button until the indicator light flashes twice.

## Conveyor System Jams & Faults
If a conveyor belt halts unexpectedly:
1. **Material Jams:** Hit the Emergency Stop (E-Stop). Visually inspect the track for polybags or loose garments caught in the rollers. Carefully remove obstructions.
2. **Tracking Problems:** If the belt is drifting to one side, this indicates misalignment. Escalate to Maintenance immediately; do not attempt to adjust tension rods.
3. **Motor Faults:** If you smell burning or hear a grinding noise, cut power entirely and radio facilities immediately.

## Forklift and Reach Truck Failures
1. **Starting Issues:** If the lift won't start, check the battery display. Ensure terminals are clean and the charging plug was fully disconnected from the warehouse dock.
2. **Hydraulic Leaks:** If you notice fluid pooling under the mast, or if the forks struggle to lift a pallet, stop use immediately and tag the lift "Out of Service". Hydraulic failures pose a severe safety hazard.

## Automated Guided Vehicle (AGV) Issues
If an AGV stops mid-aisle:
1. Check its path for physical blockages (e.g., a dropped box). AGVs will auto-halt if sensors detect an obstacle.
2. If the path is clear, check its display for "Navigation Error" or "Deadlock". If displayed, radio the Control Room to remotely refresh the AGV's virtual path links.

## When to Escalate to IT or Facilities
Escalate immediately if:
- Multiple devices drop connection simultaneously (indicating a broader network outage).
- An AGV fleet stops responding to the WMS.
- A forklift exhibits structural frame damage.
