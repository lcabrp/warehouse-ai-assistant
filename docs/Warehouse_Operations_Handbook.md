# Warehouse Operations Handbook

## Overview
This handbook governs the standard day-to-day operations across our apparel fulfillment network. All associates are expected to comply with these guidelines.

## Receiving Process
All inbound shipments must be visually inspected for damage before being logged into the WMS. Any damages must be documented with a photograph and a 'Damaged Goods' exception must be raised immediately.

## Putaway Rules
Items must be stored according to "first in-first out" (FIFO) principles. When putting away new apparel inventory, older stock must be pulled forward if the bin is shared. Ensure fabric types are stored in their designated climate zones if required.

## Picking Standards
We employ both Zone Picking (for large multi-item orders) and Wave Picking (for high-volume single-item orders). Pickers must scan the location barcode prior to scanning the item barcode to verify positional accuracy. Because apparel has high Size/Color variance, verify the SKU suffix carefully. If the quantity listed in the WMS is not physically present, the picker must log an 'Inventory Discrepancy' or 'Low Stock' exception and move to the next item in their path.

## Packing and Shipping
Packers must verify the order summary screen matches the physical items in the tote. 
1. **Garment Presentation:** Apparel must be folded neatly using the sizing templates at the packing station.
2. **Packaging:** Select the appropriate mailer or box (S, M, L) based on garment volume. Add branded tissue paper to all outbound 'Direct to Consumer' orders.
3. Orders marked as 'VIP' or 'Rush' priority skip the standard queue and must be packed immediately.

## Escalation Guidelines
Operational bottlenecks (e.g., conveyor jams, system outages) must be escalated to the Area Manager within 5 minutes of occurrence. Safety incidents must be escalated instantly.

## Safety and Equipment Handling
Only certified operators may use forklifts and pallet jacks. A 10-foot halo distance must be maintained around all moving machinery. Daily pre-check logs are mandatory for all powered equipment.
