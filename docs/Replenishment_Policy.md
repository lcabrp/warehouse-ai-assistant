# Replenishment Policy

## Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure ensures the consistent and efficient management of inventory replenishment from Bulk/Reserve storage to Forward Pick locations, minimizing stockouts and maintaining picker efficiency.

## Reorder Point Logic
Replenishment tasks are systemically triggered when the on-hand quantity in the forward pick location falls below the defined Reorder Point. 

## Safety Stock Guidelines
Safety stock is maintained at roughly 1.5x the typical Reorder Point to absorb demand spikes or supplier delays.

## Replenishment Methods
We utilize four primary replenishment strategies in the distribution center:
1. **Day/Wave Demand:** Moving exact required quantities for the day's fulfillment wave into picking slots (used for slower moving items).
2. **Top-off (Lean Time):** Filling fixed locations to maximum capacity during operational downtime to prevent mid-shift stockouts for fast-moving "A" category items.
3. **Opportunistic Replenishment:** Piggybacking replenishment tasks onto forklift operators who are already nearby to minimize empty travel time.
4. **Cross-Docking (Fast Fashion):** For highly anticipated new apparel releases, inbound containers bypass Bulk storage entirely and are transported directly to the outbound loading dock or packing area.

## Critical Shortage Escalation
If an item's status reaches 'CRITICAL' (0 quantity available) and there is no inbound stock scheduled, the picker must immediately escalate to the Inventory Control (IC) desk to initiate emergency procurement or location tracing.

## Manual Replenishment Overrides
System-generated replenishment tasks can only be manually overridden if the target location is physically damaged or unsafe to access.

## Daily Review Expectations
Supervisors must review the Replenishment Exceptions log at the end of each shift to ensure no critical tasks were abandoned.
